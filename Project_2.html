Employee Attrition

Problem Statement

This project aims to predict employee attrition at IBM using a classification model built in Python. We'll analyze employee data, such as demographics and job roles, and use algorithms like logistic regression and decision trees. The model will be evaluated for accuracy, precision, and recall, helping IBM anticipate and prevent employee attrition.


Dataset Overview

This IBM employee survey dataset includes approximately 24,000 entries, focusing on predicting attrition. The model aims to enhance the identification of at-risk employees beyond random probability. While some attrition is inevitable, minimizing it and preparing for unavoidable cases can improve business operations. Future work could involve larger datasets for more in-depth insights into attrition drivers.

Target Feature - Attrition (Categorical)

Categorical Features - Business Travel, Department, Education Field, Gender, Job Role, Marital Status, Over18, OverTime, Employee Source.

Numerical Features - Age, Daily Rate, Distance from Home, Education, Employee Count, Employee Number, Application ID, Environment Satisfaction, Hourly Rate, Job Involvement, Job Level, Job Satisfaction, Monthly Income, Monthly Rate, Number of Companies Worked, Percent Salary Hike, Performance Rating, Relationship Satisfaction, Standard Hours, Stock Option Level, Total Working Years, Training Times Last Year, Work-Life Balance, Years at Company, Years in Current Role, Years Since Last Promotion, Years with Current Manager.

Libraries and Algorithms Used:

  • Data Manipulation and Analysis: pandas, numpy
  • Mathematical Functions: math
  • Data Visualization: matplotlib, seaborn
  • Machine Learning Algorithms:
    • Linear Models: Logistic Regression, Ridge, Lasso
    • Nearest Neighbors: KNeighborsClassifier
    • Tree-Based Models: Decision Tree Classifier, Random Forest Classifier, Gradient Boosting Classifier, AdaBoost Classifier, Extra Trees Classifier
    • Support Vector Machines: SVC
    • Boosting Algorithms: XGBClassifier
  • Model Evaluation Metrics: accuracy_score, roc_auc_score, roc_curve, classification_report, mean_absolute_error, mean_squared_error, f1_score
  • Train-Test Split: train_test_split from sklearn
  • Feature Selection: RFE, SequentialFeatureSelector from sklearn
  • Handling Class Imbalance: imbalanced-learn (for SMOTE)
  • Statistical Models: statsmodels

Key Insights:

  • Removed erroneous rows and imputed missing values.
  • Adjusted column datatypes for accuracy.
  • Majority of employees are aged 30 to 40.
  • Attrition is highest among employees aged 28 to 33.
  • Male employees have a higher attrition rate.
  • Around 60% of employees work in the R&D Department.
  • The Sales department experiences a high attrition rate.
  • Female employees in the HR education field have a lower attrition rate than males.
  • Sales Representatives have the highest attrition rate for both genders.
  • Single employees show a higher attrition rate.
  • Employees with experience in only one company tend to have a higher attrition rate.
  • Attrition is higher for employees with less than 10 years of total working experience.
  • High attrition is noted among employees with fewer than 7 years since their last promotion.
  • Factors such as being single, frequent business travel, the job role of a manager, and working in the Sales department significantly contribute to employee attrition.
  • Used SMOTE to balance the dataset due to target variable imbalance.

Preprocessing Techniques Used:

  • One-Hot Encoding: OneHotEncoder from sklearn.preprocessing
  • Column Transformation: ColumnTransformer from sklearn.compose
  • Label Encoding: LabelEncoder from sklearn.preprocessing
  • Feature Scaling: StandardScaler from sklearn.preprocessing
  • Used SMOTE to balance the dataset due to target variable imbalance.

Final Conclusion:

The XGBoost classifier yielded the best performance with:

  • Train Accuracy: 99.98%
  • Test Accuracy: 99.81%
